에듀케레 Educare 고2 내신 · Self-Improvement Talks

전문 (Full Passage)

You are at a critical stage in your life, trying to figure out who you are and who you want to be. You may also be wondering how you can improve yourself. Self-improvement is not about being perfect. It is a journey to become the best version of yourself. The talks here will inspire you as you take your first steps.

Talk 1 — For Those Struggling with Creativity

I'm a writer, and I love writing. But there are moments when I feel anxious about my writing and blame myself for a lack of creativity. Creating something new is not easy, and you have probably experienced this when you had difficulty coming up with ideas for group projects or videos. So, how can we set aside those worries and enjoy the creative process more?

I did some research on this issue and found that ancient Romans understood "genius" differently from how we see it today. To them, "genius" didn't refer to a particularly gifted person but rather a divine assistant spirit of creativity. They believed that geniuses lived in the walls of artists' homes and would come out and help them with their work. This meant that if the artists' work was not good enough, they didn't have to blame themselves entirely. Everyone knew that their genius was also responsible for the quality of the work.

Some people may think this is not scientific, but we often hear stories about how people get inspired mysteriously. I once met an American poet named Ruth Stone. Throughout her life, she worked on a farm and wrote poems. She had a special way of recognizing when a poetic genius was coming to her. She said it felt like the earth was shaking under her feet! Whenever that feeling hit her, she knew she had to return home quickly and write the poem down before the genius found another poet.

My creative process isn't as dramatic as hers. I have to get up at the same time and work on my laptop for hours every day. Yet, even in this rather boring routine, I do get ideas coming from a source I can't identify. Typically, it happens when I'm deeply engaged in my work. Several hours or even days can pass, and I completely immerse myself in my creative process. It's at those moments that a brilliant idea strikes me somewhat mysteriously. I'm sure that's my genius.

Remember that you too have a magical genius that is willing to help you. But first, you must put your heart and soul into your work. Immerse yourself in your work and try to enjoy the creative process itself. If you do, your genius will come for sure.

Talk 2 — For Those Worrying about an Uncertain Future

When I was 12, I was passionate about soccer. I played soccer every day, and I always thought that I would grow up to become a great soccer player one day. Forty years later, however, I don't even watch soccer games anymore. I'm actually a big fan of American football now. My 12-year-old self may find this a betrayal.

When I was 22, I graduated from college in India with a computer science degree. At that time, it never occurred to me that I would be living a completely different life in the U.S. as a journalist and podcast host. Thirty years ago, we didn't even have podcasts or smartphones, and the future was not just unknown but unknowable.

When we look back, we can see how we have changed, but when we look forward, we tend to imagine ourselves as the same people, only older and taller. That, however, is not the case. Biologically, our cells are continuously renewing themselves, and our brains are rewiring and modifying their connections. We are constantly becoming new versions of ourselves.

Have you heard of the "Ship of Theseus"? When Theseus, a famous Greek hero, returned from his adventure of rescuing the children of Athens, the Athenians wanted to preserve his ship. They kept replacing the wood pieces that had decayed until all the original pieces were replaced with new ones. In this situation, could we still call it the Ship of Theseus?

Well, we are living examples of the Ship of Theseus. Every day, we are changing and growing to become new versions of ourselves. This means that our future selves may be very different from who we are now. Then how can we create a future we'll be proud of? I have some advice for you.

First, actively envision the kind of person you want to become in the future. To do this, spend time with different kinds of people to learn more and stay curious. Second, when expressing your views online or with friends, remember that your future self might disagree with your current opinions. So, be respectful of other people's opinions and don't just assume that you are always right. Finally, your future self will have strengths and wisdom that you may lack today. Even if you can't do something now, you might be able to in the future. So, don't be afraid of new challenges. Be brave!

  • 강조 부사 even: 부정문에서 「~조차도」의 강조. don't even watch = 보지조차 않다.
  • 부사 anymore: 부정문에서 「더 이상 (~하지 않다)」.
35I'm actually a big fan of American football now.
I'm actually a big fan of American football now.
사실 저는 지금 미식축구의 열렬한 팬입니다.
  • 강조 부사 actually: 앞의 내용과 대조되는 사실을 강조(「사실은」).
36My 12-year-old self may find this a betrayal.
My 12-year-old self / may find this a betrayal.
12살의 나는 이것을 배신이라고 생각할지도 모릅니다.
  • 5형식(find) find + O + OC: find this a betrayal = 이것을 배신으로 생각하다.
  • 조동사(추측) may find: ~라고 생각할지도 모른다(약한 추측).
37When I was 22, I graduated from college in India with a computer science degree.
When I was 22, / I graduated from college in India / with a computer science degree.
22살 때, 컴퓨터 과학 학위를 취득하며 인도에서 대학을 졸업했습니다.
  • 부대상황 with with a computer science degree: 동시 상황·결과를 나타내는 with + 명사.
38At that time, it never occurred to me that I would be living a completely different life in the U.S. as a journalist and podcast host.
At that time, / it never occurred to me / that I would be living a completely different life / in the U.S. as a journalist and podcast host.
그 당시에는, 미국에서 기자이자 팟캐스트 진행자로서 완전히 다른 삶을 살게 될 것이라는 생각이 전혀 들지 않았습니다.
  • 비인칭 it it occurred to me that ~: 「~라는 생각이 (나에게) 떠올랐다」관용 표현.
  • 미래진행(과거기준) would be living: 과거 시점에서 바라본 미래의 진행 상황.
  • 강조 부사 never: 완전 부정(전혀 ~않다).
39Thirty years ago, we didn't even have podcasts or smartphones, and the future was not just unknown but unknowable.
Thirty years ago, / we didn't even have podcasts or smartphones, / and the future was not just unknown / but unknowable.
30년 전에는 팟캐스트나 스마트폰조차 없었고, 미래는 그저 알 수 없는 것이 아니라 인식 자체가 불가능한 것이었습니다.
  • 강조 부사 didn't even have: ~조차도 없었다.
  • 상관접속사 not just A but B: A만이 아니라 B이기도 하다. 강조 구문.
40When we look back, we can see how we have changed, but when we look forward, we tend to imagine ourselves as the same people, only older and taller.
When we look back, / we can see how we have changed, / but when we look forward, / we tend to imagine ourselves as the same people, / only older and taller.
뒤를 돌아볼 때 우리는 어떻게 변해왔는지 알 수 있지만, 앞을 바라볼 때 우리는 자신을 단지 더 늙고 키가 큰 같은 사람으로 상상하는 경향이 있습니다.
  • 현재완료 have changed: 과거부터 현재까지의 변화(완료 용법).
  • tend to-V tend to imagine: ~하는 경향이 있다.
  • 5형식 imagine ourselves as the same people: imagine + O + as C.
41That, however, is not the case.
That, however, / is not the case.
그러나 그것은 사실이 아닙니다.
  • 관용표현 not the case: 사실이 아니다(= not true). That is not the case = That is wrong.
  • 삽입어구 however: 접속부사. 앞 문장과 대조를 나타내며 콤마로 삽입.
42Biologically, our cells are continuously renewing themselves, and our brains are rewiring and modifying their connections.
Biologically, / our cells are continuously renewing themselves, / and our brains are rewiring and modifying their connections.
생물학적으로, 우리의 세포는 지속적으로 스스로를 새롭게 하고 있으며, 뇌는 신경 연결을 재구성하고 수정하고 있습니다.
  • 재귀대명사 renewing themselves: 주어(cells)와 동일 대상.
  • 현재진행 are continuously renewing / are rewiring: 지속 중인 변화 강조.
43We are constantly becoming new versions of ourselves.
We are constantly becoming / new versions of ourselves.
우리는 끊임없이 새로운 버전의 자신이 되어가고 있습니다.
  • 현재진행(지속) are constantly becoming: 현재진행 + 빈도 부사 constantly로 지속·변화 강조.
  • 재귀대명사 ourselves: 주어(We)와 동일 대상.
44Have you heard of the "Ship of Theseus"?
Have you heard of / the "Ship of Theseus"?
"테세우스의 배"에 대해 들어본 적이 있나요?
  • 현재완료(경험) Have you heard of ~?: 과거부터 현재까지의 경험을 묻는 의문문.
  • hear of heard of: ~에 대해 들어본 적이 있다(= hear about).
45When Theseus, a famous Greek hero, returned from his adventure of rescuing the children of Athens, the Athenians wanted to preserve his ship.
When Theseus, a famous Greek hero, / returned from his adventure / of rescuing the children of Athens, / the Athenians wanted to preserve his ship.
유명한 그리스 영웅 테세우스가 아테네 아이들을 구출하는 모험에서 돌아왔을 때, 아테네 시민들은 그의 배를 보존하고 싶었습니다.
  • 동격 a famous Greek hero: 주어 Theseus를 설명하는 동격 명사구.
  • 전치사+동명사 of rescuing the children: 명사 adventure를 수식하는 전치사구.
46They kept replacing the wood pieces that had decayed until all the original pieces were replaced with new ones.
They kept replacing / the wood pieces that had decayed / until all the original pieces were replaced with new ones.
그들은 원래의 조각들이 모두 새것으로 교체될 때까지 썩은 나무 조각들을 계속 교체했습니다.
  • keep -ing kept replacing: ~을 계속하다(지속·반복).
  • 관계대명사+과거완료 that had decayed: 선행사 wood pieces를 수식. 과거완료로 주절보다 먼저 일어난 일.
  • 수동태 were replaced with: replace A with B(A를 B로 교체하다)의 수동.
47In this situation, could we still call it the Ship of Theseus?
In this situation, / could we still call it / the Ship of Theseus?
이 상황에서, 우리는 여전히 그것을 테세우스의 배라고 부를 수 있을까요?
  • 5형식(call) call + O + OC: it(목적어)을 the Ship of Theseus(목적보어)라고 부르다.
  • 수사의문문 could we still call ~?: 독자에게 철학적 질문을 던지는 수사의문문.
48Well, we are living examples of the Ship of Theseus.
Well, we are living examples / of the Ship of Theseus.
글쎄요, 우리는 테세우스의 배의 살아있는 예입니다.
  • 현재분사(형용사) living examples: living은 명사 examples를 수식하는 형용사적 현재분사(「살아있는」).
49Every day, we are changing and growing to become new versions of ourselves.
Every day, / we are changing and growing / to become new versions of ourselves.
매일, 우리는 자신의 새로운 버전이 되기 위해 변화하고 성장하고 있습니다.
  • 병렬구조 changing and growing: 두 현재분사가 and로 병렬.
  • 부사적 to부정사(목적) to become new versions: ~이 되기 위하여(목적).
50This means that our future selves may be very different from who we are now.
This means / that our future selves / may be very different from who we are now.
이것은 미래의 우리 자신이 지금의 우리와 매우 다를 수 있다는 것을 의미합니다.
  • that절(목적어) means that ~: 동사 mean의 목적어절.
  • 관계대명사 what who we are now: 전치사 from의 목적어. who = the person that(= 지금의 우리 자신).
51Then how can we create a future we'll be proud of?
Then how can we create / a future / we'll be proud of?
그렇다면 우리가 자랑스러워할 미래를 어떻게 만들 수 있을까요?
  • 관계대명사 생략 a future (that) we'll be proud of: 목적격 관계대명사 생략. 전치사 of가 관계절 끝에 위치.
  • be proud of be proud of: ~을 자랑스러워하다.
52First, actively envision the kind of person you want to become in the future.
First, / actively envision / the kind of person / you want to become in the future.
첫째, 미래에 되고 싶은 사람의 모습을 적극적으로 그려 보십시오.
  • 명령문 envision ~: 동사원형으로 시작하는 명령문(조언·권고).
  • 관계대명사 생략 the kind of person (that/whom) you want to become: 목적격 관계대명사 생략.
53To do this, spend time with different kinds of people to learn more and stay curious.
To do this, / spend time with different kinds of people / to learn more and stay curious.
이를 위해, 더 많이 배우고 호기심을 유지하기 위해 다양한 종류의 사람들과 시간을 보내세요.
  • 부사적 to부정사(목적) To do this: 「이것을 하기 위해」. 문두에서 목적을 제시.
  • 부사적 to부정사(목적) to learn more and stay curious: ~하기 위하여. 두 동사가 and로 병렬.
54Second, when expressing your views online or with friends, remember that your future self might disagree with your current opinions.
Second, when expressing your views online or with friends, / remember that / your future self might disagree / with your current opinions.
둘째, 온라인이나 친구들과 의견을 표현할 때, 미래의 자신이 현재 의견에 동의하지 않을 수 있음을 기억하세요.
  • 분사구문(시간) when expressing your views: when + 현재분사. 주어 생략(= when you express).
  • 명령문+that절 remember that ~: that절이 remember의 목적어.
  • 조동사(추측) might disagree: ~할 수도 있다(약한 가능성).
55So, be respectful of other people's opinions and don't just assume that you are always right.
So, be respectful of other people's opinions / and don't just assume / that you are always right.
그러므로, 다른 사람들의 의견을 존중하고 자신이 항상 옳다고 단정하지 마세요.
  • 명령문(병렬) be respectful … and don't assume: 긍정 명령과 부정 명령이 and로 병렬.
  • be respectful of be respectful of ~: ~을 존중하다.
  • that절(목적어) assume that ~: 동사 assume의 목적어절.
56Finally, your future self will have strengths and wisdom that you may lack today.
Finally, your future self / will have strengths and wisdom / that you may lack today.
마지막으로, 미래의 당신은 오늘 여러분이 부족할 수도 있는 강점과 지혜를 갖게 될 것입니다.
  • 주격 관계대명사 strengths and wisdom that you may lack: 선행사 strengths and wisdom를 수식.
  • 조동사(추측) may lack: ~이 부족할 수도 있다(현재 추측).
57Even if you can't do something now, you might be able to in the future.
Even if you can't do something now, / you might be able to (do it) in the future.
지금 당장 무언가를 할 수 없더라도, 미래에는 할 수 있을지도 모릅니다.
  • 양보 접속사 even if: 「비록 ~하더라도」(가정적 양보).
  • 대동사 생략 might be able to (do it): 앞 내용의 반복을 피한 생략.
58So, don't be afraid of new challenges.
So, don't be afraid of new challenges.
그러니, 새로운 도전을 두려워하지 마세요.
  • 부정 명령문 don't be afraid of: Don't + 동사원형. be afraid of ~: ~을 두려워하다.
59Be brave!
Be brave!
용감해지세요!
  • 명령문 Be brave: Be + 형용사 형태의 명령문. 글 전체의 핵심 메시지를 한 문장으로 압축.

창의성은 완전히 자신에게서 오는 것이 아니므로, 결과에 지나치게 자책하지 말고 작업에 몰입하면 영감은 자연히 찾아온다.
Talk 2 요지 (Main Idea)
인간은 끊임없이 변화하는 존재이므로, 미래의 자신이 지금과 매우 달라질 것임을 인식하고 호기심·겸손·용기를 갖추어 미래를 만들어 나가야 한다.

③ 사례 제시: Ruth Stone 시인의 영감을 맞이하는 방식
④ 화자의 경험: 몰입 속에서 찾아오는 영감
⑤ 결론·조언: 작업에 몰입하면 천재(영감)는 반드시 온다

① 개인 경험: 12세 축구 열정 → 40년 후 미식축구 팬(변화의 사례)
② 미래 예측의 한계: 과거를 돌아보면 변화가 보이나, 미래는 unknowable
③ 과학적 근거: 세포 재생, 뇌 신경망 재구성 → 인간은 계속 변화

Talk 1 핵심 소재 비교
고대 로마의 genius vs. 현대인의 인식

글의 순서 5. Talk 1에서 주어진 문장 다음에 이어질 글의 순서로 가장 적절한 것은?

[주어진 문장] I did some research on this issue and found that ancient Romans understood "genius" differently from how we see it today.

(A) I once met an American poet named Ruth Stone. She had a special way of recognizing when a poetic genius was coming to her.
(B) To them, "genius" didn't refer to a particularly gifted person but rather a divine assistant spirit of creativity.
(C) This meant that if the artists' work was not good enough, they didn't have to blame themselves entirely.
주어진 문장에서 로마인들의 genius 개념을 언급 → (B) genius의 정의 설명(divine assistant spirit) → (C) 그 결과(자책 불필요) → (A) 구체적 사례(Ruth Stone) 순서가 자연스럽습니다.

서술형·영작 (Descriptive & Writing)

1. [빈칸 요약] Talk 2의 핵심 내용을 다음과 같이 요약할 때, (A)와 (B)에 들어갈 말을 지문에서 찾아 쓰시오.
조건: 각각 한 단어로, 지문에 나온 표현 그대로 쓸 것

Humans are living examples of the Ship of Theseus. We are constantly (A)__________ new versions of ourselves, which means our future selves may be very different. Therefore, we should actively (B)__________ the kind of person we want to become.

(A) becoming — "We are constantly becoming new versions of ourselves"에서 직접 가져옴.
(B) envision — "actively envision the kind of person you want to become"에서 직접 가져옴.
2. [내용 서술] Talk 1에서 화자가 소개하는 고대 로마인의 'genius' 개념을 우리말로 설명하고, 이 개념이 창의적 작업을 하는 사람들에게 어떤 심리적 의미를 가지는지 서술하시오.
조건: ① 'genius'의 로마적 의미를 포함할 것 ② 심리적 효과를 구체적으로 쓸 것 ③ 60자 이상 80자 이내의 우리말로 쓸 것
고대 로마인들에게 'genius'는 예술가의 집에 사는 창의성의 신성한 조력자 정신을 의미했다. 이 개념은 작품의 질이 낮을 때 예술가가 온전히 자책하지 않아도 된다는 심리적 안정감을 제공한다.

※ 핵심 요소: ① genius = 신성한 조력자 정신(divine assistant spirit) ② 자책 감소 효과
3. [영작] 다음 우리말을 조건에 맞게 영작하시오.

"비록 지금 당장 무언가를 할 수 없더라도, 미래에는 할 수 있을지도 모른다."
조건: ① even if를 사용할 것 ② might be able to를 사용할 것 ③ 9단어 이상으로 쓸 것
Even if you can't do something now, you might be able to in the future.

※ 대동사 생략(might be able to do it → might be able to) 허용. can't do → cannot do 모두 정답 처리.
4. [어법 교정] 다음 문장의 밑줄 친 부분 중 어법상 틀린 것을 찾아 번호를 쓰고 올바르게 고쳐 쓰시오.

It ①never occurred to me ②that I ③would live ④a completely different life in the U.S. ⑤as a journalist.
조건: 번호와 함께 틀린 표현 → 올바른 표현 형식으로 쓸 것
③ would live → would be living

과거의 특정 시점(그 당시)에서 바라본 미래의 진행 중인 상황을 나타내므로 미래진행(would be living)이 적절합니다. 원문: "I would be living a completely different life"
